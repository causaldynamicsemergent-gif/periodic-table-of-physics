How to read this periodic table

What you're looking at. Each tile is a formal classification — a Mendeleev-style partition of physical or mathematical structure. Rows are sectors of physics; tiles within a row are the classifications in that sector.

Tile anatomy

The big 2–3 letter mark is the classification's symbol (SM, GUT, 10W for ten-fold way, MGr for modified gravity, …). The left-edge stripe is the category colour: blue = structural, green = phenomenon, amber = hybrid. Top-right shows cell count; if there are falsified predictions inside, you'll see a red ⚠. The thin bar at the bottom is the predictive yield: green = confirmed, amber = tension, gray = not yet tested, red = falsified, purple = retro-explanatory.

Drill-down

Hover a tile for a tooltip preview. Click a tile to open its details in the right sidebar — description, classification axes, cells, predictions (with a status filter), cross-classification edges, citations. The periodic table stays visible the whole time. Inside the sidebar, you can click any cell to see its full record (axis values, examples, predictions tied to that cell, citations). A breadcrumb at the top of the sidebar lets you walk back up.

Physical phenomena

Click the Phenomena button at the top of the sidebar (or open Browse → Physical phenomena). Each toggle highlights the classifications that contain that thing, in its category's colour. A tile glows with one ring per matching category — so a tile hit by both Particles and Cosmological will show both rings. Phenomena that map to the same exact set of classifications have been combined; the merging itself is information. all on / all off reset.

Finding a specific thing

The search box at the top of the toolbar searches all 484 cells. Type black holes, gluons, AdS/CFT, topological insulator, or anything else. Results are grouped by classification with cell snippets; clicking a result opens that exact cell. For the terms used to describe how the map itself is organised (open frontier, discharge status, candidate-foundational, …) open the Glossary via the sidebar quick-bar or Browse menu.

Layers + filters

Rows by reorganises the table: by sector (default), category (structural/hybrid/phenomenon), or closure level. Spotlight dims tiles whose predictions don't match a status. Overlay: phen↔phen edges draws arrows on the map showing the 7 phenomenon-to-phenomenon relationships, with compact-astrophysical-objects as the visual hub.

Map controls

Drag anywhere on the map background to pan. Scroll / pinch over the map to zoom (cursor-anchored). Buttons in the top-left: + / − step zoom, ⟲ fits the whole map to your viewport. Keyboard: + / − step zoom, 0 or f fit to view, Esc to clear a selection. The sidebar collapses with the › tab on its left edge.

What's in the data

30 formal classifications · 484 cells · 209 predictions (223 including cell-scoped) · 97 cross-classification edges · 17 falsifications · 7 phenomenon-to-phenomenon edges. The dataset is fetched live from the GitHub repo at page load.
