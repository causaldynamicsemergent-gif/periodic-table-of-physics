# Using the Map of Physics

This directory is the **use-side** of the project: physicist-facing material that shows what the curated catalogue is good for and how to ask it questions. Where `/methodology/` documents how the catalogue is built and governed (maintainer-facing), `/use/` is written for a working physicist or graduate student arriving for the first time.

Everything here is physics-first: you ask in the vocabulary of a colloquium or a *Physical Review* paper, and the answers come back the same way. Every claim in these documents traces to an entry you can open and check.

---

## What's here

### Worked sessions — [`sessions/`](sessions/)

Curated end-to-end walkthroughs, each verified against the live data. Each takes a question a physicist might actually have, shows the move that answers it, and closes with a reusable recipe.

- **[`sessions/leptogenesis.md`](sessions/leptogenesis.md)** — *What does the catalogue say about baryogenesis?* The frontier-level prediction move end to end: read the baryon-asymmetry frontier (η ≈ 6.1 × 10⁻¹⁰, the Standard Model falling quantitatively short), follow the leptogenesis resolution into its forced consequences — the neutrino-sector Dirac-vs-Majorana cell forced to Majorana (carrying M₁ ≳ 10⁹ GeV, Davidson–Ibarra) and the leptogenesis cell promoted to confirmed-by-indirect-evidence — and its single bounds-setting resolver, next-generation electron-EDM searches.

- **[`sessions/strong-cp.md`](sessions/strong-cp.md)** — *What does the catalogue say about the strong-CP problem?* A frontier with two independent bounds-setting channels — axion haloscopes bounding $g_{a\gamma\gamma}$ and next-generation neutron-EDM searches bounding $\bar\theta_{\mathrm{QCD}}$ — addressing the same Peccei–Quinn resolution from complementary directions, plus an honest reading of why the discriminating-experiments question comes back empty, and the QCD-axion classification a detection would force.

*(Next-generation EDM searches appear in both sessions — neutron-EDM for strong-CP, electron-EDM for leptogenesis — which makes that program the catalogue's first dual-frontier experimental program.)*

### How to use the map — [`recipes.md`](recipes.md)

A "How to use the map" page: the query patterns from the worked sessions, distilled into a paragraph each, for a physicist who wants to try a question of their own. No prior experience with the underlying server assumed.

### The essay — `essay.md` *(forthcoming)*

## How to read these

Start with a session that opens on a question you recognize, then skim [`recipes.md`](recipes.md) for the general patterns. The sessions are the demonstration; the recipes are the scaffolding for your own questions; the essay (when it lands) is the framing for why the whole thing is worth your time.
