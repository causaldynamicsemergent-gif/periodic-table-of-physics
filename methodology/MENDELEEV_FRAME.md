## §1. The Mendeleev frame — the substrate as a configuration space

The project's name suggests a single periodic-table-of-physics analog. The substrate's structure is more interesting than that: it isn't a single table; it is a **network of projections connected by derivational structure**. Each of the 30 formal classifications is one pre-authored projection — sm-rep-content organized by gauge-representation × generation, dark-matter-candidates by candidate-class × production-mechanism × observational-status, qcd-phase-diagram by temperature × baryon-chemical-potential, and so on. The cross-classification edge layer (with subtypes derives-from, specializes, constrains, bears-on, uses-classification, produces-classification) is the network connecting these projections. The "periodic table of physics" appropriate to a given physics question is the *configuration* of projections selected for that question, not a fixed master table.

Four moves operate over this configuration space. **M1** — empty-cell prediction: the substrate makes the properties of unfilled positions derivable from their surroundings before any specific candidate is named. **M2** — cross-classification recurrence: literature-anchored content appearing at corresponding positions across projections is the periodicity analog and the substrate-enabled toolkit M1's prediction work depends on. **M3** — testing candidate frameworks: candidate-foundational programs claiming to reorganize or unify the structural network record their commitments in the substrate, against which the physicist tests them. **M4** — experimental-program adjudication: running and near-term programs targeting open questions are surfaced with their sensitivity, timeline, and per-program competing predictions where they exist.

M1 (mode 1) and M4 are **substrate-contained**: the map directly holds the relevant content; the physicist looks it up. M1 (modes 2–3), M2, and M3 are **substrate-enabled**: the map provides literature-anchored organization on which the physicist's pattern-recognition, derivation, and adjudication work runs. The distinction matters for surface honesty. Mendeleev didn't invent the elements — he organized literature-anchored empirical data on them so the periodicity became visible to himself and to other working chemists. The same structural relationship applies here.

## §2. M1 — empty-cell prediction

**Periodic-table analog.** The table predicts what occupies an unfilled position by reading off the position's structural surroundings — atomic weight from the column, valence from the row, expected chemical behavior from neighboring elements — *before* any specific candidate is found or named. Eka-aluminum's atomic weight ~68 and density ~5.9 were predicted from the table's structure; gallium was discovered later to match.

The load-bearing claim of M1 is the same: **the properties of an empty cell are derivable from its structural surroundings before any candidate is named**. The substrate organizes the surroundings; the physicist performs the derivation; named candidates the literature has proposed are then evaluated against the derived properties.

M1 operates in three modes, corresponding to how the empty cell enters view.

**Mode 1 — named-frontier-first (substrate-contained).** A known open question is the entry point. The substrate has 11 open-frontier nodes (hierarchy problem, strong-CP problem, cosmological constant, quantum gravity, matter-antimatter asymmetry, dark matter, measurement problem, flavor puzzle, and others) each carrying: the empirical bound any resolution must satisfy; a structural-reason category for why the math breaks down at this position; the literature-named candidate resolutions, each with a discharge status rating its current viability; and for each candidate, what would be forced structurally if that resolution were real — typically a new classification, a new axis on an existing one, a forced cross-classification edge, or a specific quantitative scale — with literature citations on each implication. The map directly holds all of this. The physicist's work is to navigate the structural neighborhood (the formal classifications bearing on the frontier, the cells in those classifications most directly constraining the resolution) and derive further properties beyond what the substrate records.

*Worked instance.* The hierarchy-problem open-frontier node carries a 17-orders-of-magnitude empirical bound between the electroweak (~246 GeV) and Planck (~10¹⁹ GeV) scales; the structural reason that QFT loop corrections to the Higgs mass diverge quadratically and require unnatural cancellation at SM scales; five named candidate resolutions (TeV-scale supersymmetry, composite Higgs, Randall-Sundrum extra dimensions, anthropic landscape, cosmological relaxion), each rated against LHC and precision-electroweak constraints; and for each candidate, the forced structural consequences — TeV-scale supersymmetry forces a new classification organizing superpartner content at m_SUSY ≲ 1 TeV (Martin 1997; Barbieri-Giudice 1988); composite Higgs forces a new classification organizing strong-sector content at compositeness scale f ≲ 1 TeV (Panico-Wulzer 2016; Contino 2010); and so on. A physicist landing on this node has the empty cell partly characterized before opening any candidate's case: the resolution must be active at scale ≲ TeV; it must structurally cancel quadratic divergences rather than fine-tune them away; it must reproduce the 246-GeV electroweak vacuum and respect existing constraint data. *These are the empty cell's properties, derived from the surroundings.*

**Mode 2 — pairwise comparison (substrate-enabled).** The entry point is not a named question but a comparison the physicist constructs between two formal classifications along a shared content axis or a shared cross-cutting concept. Empty cells emerge from the alignment — positions where the two classifications' axes generate a slot that neither's pre-authored content fills. The substrate enables the comparison through cross-classification edges, cross-cutting concept indexing, and `compare_classifications`; the physicist constructs the alignment and reads the empty cells off it.

**Mode 3 — multi-face configuration (substrate-enabled).** The entry point is a configuration the physicist constructs by selecting axes from N classifications simultaneously — for instance, a stellar-emission ↔ subatomic-particle configuration spanning electromagnetic-phenomena, sm-rep-content, and compact-astrophysical-objects, with axes drawn from each. Empty cells in the constructed configuration become visible, and may be visible only in this configuration. The substrate's job in mode 3 is **focus-not-filter**: the constructed configuration foregrounds the physicist's question, but the substrate's derivational structure (the edges connecting the constructed cells to their upstream architectures, the shared structural origins of subsets of cells) remains visible in the background. Without this discipline, mode-3 configurations risk surfacing patterns that are artifacts of a chosen projection — recurrences that emerge because the configuration is shadowing a deeper shared derivation rather than because of independent periodicity. With it, an empty cell in the constructed configuration is empty *given* the substrate's known derivational structure — the Mendeleev-faithful version of the move.

**Physicist-facing prose** (for downstream sourcing). The Map of Physics organizes settled physics so that what is missing can be characterized before it is found. Open questions — the hierarchy problem, the strong-CP problem, dark matter's particle identity — are recorded as nodes carrying the empirical bound any resolution must satisfy, the structural reason the current theory breaks down at that position, the candidate resolutions the literature has named, and what each would force if it turned out to be the right answer. The same move operates at smaller scale across the substrate's 30 formal classifications: cells with named candidates and pending empirical resolution sit next to cells with established content, and the structural surroundings constrain what the missing piece must look like.

**Tools.** Mode 1: `get_node` on an open-frontier node, `find_signal_implications`, `find_bears_on`, `find_targets_of_program`. Modes 2–3: `find_cross_classification`, `compare_classifications`, `find_cells` with content filters, navigation across the substrate's projections via cross-cutting concept indexing. Two narrower encodings of the same move are also substrate-supported as limiting cases: conjectured cross-classification edges with `targeted_by` (currently one — the SU(5) embedding) and `conjectured-by-pattern` cells in formal classifications (currently unpopulated; the strictest schema-level encoding, architecturally supported but empirically rare).

## §3. M2 — cross-classification recurrence

**Periodic-table analog.** When literature-anchored content appears at corresponding positions across multiple formal classifications, the recurrence is the periodicity itself — the same physical content appearing in two or more projections of the substrate.

M2 plays two roles.

**Recurrence-recording.** Where the literature has explicitly noted a cross-classification correspondence — has named both the classifications and the cells that correspond — the substrate records the correspondence as a cross-classification edge with status (established / partial / conjectured), subtype, cell_refs pinning the corresponding cells, and citations to the literature anchoring the correspondence. These edges are substrate-contained outputs of literature-anchored synthesis; the map does not synthesize across literature-anchored fragments to invent recurrences.

**Recurrence-discovery (substrate-enabled).** M2's central role in the workflow is as **M1's toolkit**. The substrate's organized cross-classification structure — the recorded edges, the cross-cutting concept arrays linking cells with shared content across classifications, the relationship indexing across formal-classification records — is what enables a physicist's M1 mode-2 and mode-3 work to be tractable. Constructing a productive comparison between projections requires knowing which content cross-cuts which classifications; the substrate provides that organization. Discovering a previously-unrecorded recurrence is the physicist's work, performed against the substrate's literature-anchored material.

**Worked instance.** The `edge-xc-crit-specializes-bootstrap` edge records that real critical systems — liquid-gas critical points in H₂O, CO₂, Xe; antiferromagnetic transitions in FeF₂, MnF₂; the ⁴He λ-transition; the 3D-Heisenberg transition in EuO, RbMnF₃ — land on critical exponents that conformal-bootstrap exclusion regions independently pin to high precision (3D-Ising ν = 0.6299709...; 3D O(2); 3D O(3)). Citations El-Showk et al. 2014, Kos-Poland-Simmons-Duffin 2014, Chester et al. 2020/2021 anchor the correspondence in the literature. The substrate records the recurrence; the broader pattern — "structural-content classifications constraining phenomenon classifications" recurring elsewhere — is the physicist's discovery work, performed against the substrate's organized material.

**Physicist-facing prose.** When the same physical content sits at corresponding positions in multiple classifications of the substrate, the recurrence is the periodicity analog. Literature-anchored correspondences are recorded directly as connections in the map; further pattern discovery — constructing comparisons that surface recurrences the literature has not yet codified — is the physicist's work, with the map providing the organized structural material it runs on.

**Tools.** `find_cross_classification` (with status, subtype, from, to filters), `compare_classifications`, `find_cells` with content filters and cross-cutting concept search, per-classification record fetches via `get_classification`.

## §4. M3 — testing candidate frameworks

**Periodic-table analog.** A candidate atomic theory is tested against whether it reproduces the table's periodicity and accommodates its content. M3 is the analog at framework scale: candidate-foundational programs claiming to reorganize, unify, or extend the structural network are tested against whether their commitments are consistent with the substrate's literature-anchored organization.

**Substrate location.** Candidate-foundational frameworks live in the substrate as **architecture nodes with `empirical_status: candidate-foundational`** — the SU(5) GUT program, the AdS/CFT correspondence, noncommutative-geometry / Connes spectral action, causal-set theory, tensor-network architectures, and others. Each records `candidate_targeting` entries (the open frontiers it claims to address, with a discharge_status) and is the `targeted_by` source on the conjectured-status cross-classification edges encoding its structural claims. The substrate records *what the program claims*; whether the claims hold is the physicist's test, performed against the rest of the substrate and the literature.

**Worked instance.** The SU(5) GUT program is an architecture node with `empirical_status: candidate-foundational`. It targets the hierarchy problem (discharge status: partial — minimal SU(5) does not solve the EW-GUT hierarchy without SUSY layered on top) and claims a structural commitment recorded as `edge-xc-sm-specializes-su5-gut` (status conjectured, targeted_by su5-gut-program, 18 cell_refs pinning the SM cells whose 5̄ + 10 embedding into SU(5) representations is the program's specific structural claim, citations Georgi-Glashow 1974 and Buras-Ellis-Gaillard-Nanopoulos 1978). The substrate does not assert that the embedding holds. It records the program's commitment, anchored to the literature that proposed it. The proton-decay experimental record sits in a different classification (proton-decay-searches) with `cell-pd-pi0-eplus-falsified-GG` recording the Super-Kamiokande falsification of the minimal-Georgi-Glashow variant. The adjudication — minimal non-SUSY SU(5) is falsified, SUSY-SU(5) and SO(10) variants remain conjectured-but-viable — is performed by the literature against the substrate's record, not by the project.

**Physicist-facing prose.** Candidate frameworks for reorganizing or unifying physics — proposed grand unifications, holographic dualities, and other foundational programs — sit alongside the structural classifications they make claims about. Each program records what it commits to: which open questions it addresses, with what current standing; what structural relationships it claims; what would follow if it were correct. A physicist testing a framework against the rest of the substrate can compare its commitments with the literature-anchored organization the map records, and consult the experimental record for adjudication where it exists.

**Tools.** `find_targets_of_program`, `find_hosting`, `find_targeting`, `get_axis_mapping`, `find_uses_classification`, `find_produces_classification`, `find_cross_classification` with status filters and `targeted_by` matching.

## §5. M4 — experimental-program adjudication

**Periodic-table analog.** Empirical measurements adjudicate where predictions land. M4 is the substrate's recording of the running and near-term experimental programs bearing on open questions, with their sensitivity, timeline, and where competing point-predictive theoretical programs exist, side-by-side adjudicable predictions.

**Substrate-contained.** 19 experimental-program nodes (7 historical, 12 forward-looking) connect to formal classifications, open frontiers, and totality-approach nodes through 40 resolves edges. Each edge carries a sensitivity (a quantitative-scale entry with kind, value, units, bound direction, design-document citations), a timeline (running / planned / proposed), an `exclusion_only` flag distinguishing bounds-setting programs from adjudicating programs, and a `predictions_per_program` array populated when competing theoretical programs predict different point values the experiment can discriminate between.

**Worked instance.** The strong-CP problem open frontier carries two resolves edges from independent experimental programs. The axion-haloscope network (ADMX, HAYSTAC, MADMAX, ABRACADABRA, DMRadio) directly searches for the QCD axion via cavity-photon conversion, with sensitivity g_aγγ ≲ 10⁻¹⁵ GeV⁻¹ (upper bound, ADMX Run 1B Bartram et al. 2021). The next-generation electric-dipole-moment program (n2EDM at PSI, ACME, JILA HfF⁺) indirectly probes residual θ̄_QCD via the Crewther-DiVecchia-Veneziano-Witten translation from |d_n| to θ̄, with n2EDM design sensitivity |d_n| ≲ 10⁻²⁷ e·cm (upper bound, Ayres et al. 2021). Both are bounds-setting; the dual-channel structure probes the same Peccei-Quinn-axion resolution from complementary directions — direct axion-photon coupling and indirect bound on the order parameter the PQ mechanism dynamically relaxes. The `find_discriminating_experiments` query correctly returns empty for both programs because no competing point-predictive resolution exists at the experimentally-accessible level — the firewall-faithful posture, not a tool limitation.

**Physicist-facing prose.** The map records which running and near-term experimental programs bear on each open question, at what sensitivity, on what timeline. Where competing theoretical programs predict different values the experiment can discriminate between, those predictions appear side-by-side on the edge. Where there is one underlying number nature has fixed and the program measures or excludes it, the edge is recorded as bounds-setting. A physicist asking "what's the current and near-term empirical coverage of this question?" gets the catalog with its sensitivities and timelines anchored to design documents.

**Tools.** `find_resolvers`, `find_discriminating_experiments`, `find_predictions`.

## §6. The ordering principle

M1 leads. It is where the substrate's value reads off most directly: a physicist's first interaction with the map should be with an open-frontier node carrying its empirical bound, structural reason, candidate resolutions, and forced consequences — together with the configuration-space and comparison surfaces that let the physicist construct further empty-cell views for their own questions. M2 is M1's toolkit; the two are integrated workflow rather than parallel sibling moves. M3 sits next to M1: when a physicist has named or constructed the empty cell, candidate frameworks claiming to fill it are tested against the surrounding structure the substrate organizes. M4 is downstream — empirical adjudication of what the structural work has surfaced.

A fresh reader landing on M4 first reads the project as an experimental-program reference. A fresh reader landing on M1 first reads it as a periodic table whose surrounding structure makes predictions about what is missing and that also tracks experimental coverage. The data supports both readings; the positioning decides which one. The Mendeleev claim implicit in the project's name is the M1 reading; the surfaces must back the claim.

## §7. Explorer architecture commitment

The current explorer surface is the FC tile grid — 30 settled-physics classifications presented with axis labels, cell content, and yield bars. The presentation is accurate but inert. A working physicist landing on the grid has no entry point for use: there is no view that says *here is what is missing, here is what it must look like to be filled, here is what is already known about the surroundings*. The result is the diagnostic question — *so what? what am I supposed to do with this?*

The frame doc commits to the following explorer architecture; downstream sub-PRs (the E0 sequence in `MENDELEEV_POSITIONING_HANDOFF.md` §3.1, with subsequent reshape) source from this commitment.

**Parallel top-level views, toggleable from the toolbar.** One view: *open questions* — the 11 open-frontier nodes as primary cards, each carrying empirical bound, structural reason, named candidate resolutions, forced consequences, and the structural neighborhood (bears-on edges, adjacent classifications, the cells most directly constraining what fills the empty cell). The other view: *structural classifications* — the existing FC tile grid, extended with the constructive-status visual treatment for forbidden and conjectured cells. Either view can be the default landing.

**Comparison panel, invokable from either view.** A physicist selects two classifications (or starts from a cross-cutting concept), the explorer assembles their cells along common axes or shared content, and constructed empty positions become visible. M1 mode-2 support.

**Configuration builder, invokable from either view.** A physicist selects axes from N classifications simultaneously; the explorer composes a constructed multi-face configuration; empty cells visible only in that configuration surface. M1 mode-3 support.

**Focus-not-filter discipline.** In every comparison and configuration, the substrate's derivational structure — the cross-classification edges connecting the foreground cells to their upstream architectures — remains visible in the background as faded indicators and a peripheral panel. When the substrate detects that the foreground cells share an upstream structural origin, an automatic note surfaces so the physicist does not mistake a downstream artifact for an independent recurrence. This is a binding design principle, not a polish item; without it, the constructed configurations are not epistemically safe and the M1 move is not Mendeleev-faithful in modes 2–3.

**Downstream data-layer finding flagged.** The focus-not-filter discipline makes the derivation / specializes / bears-on edge layer load-bearing for the explorer UI. Any unauthored derivational structure becomes a hole the explorer cannot signal. A derivation-edge audit becomes a Track 5 housekeeping line, downstream of explorer surfacing — independent of the frame doc's commitments but a consequence the maintainer should track.

## §8. The four binding disciplines

The four disciplines are independent and all must hold.

The **firewall** (`META_v21_1_methodology_firewall.md`) binds the data layer: pattern-finding does not drive authoring; authoring decisions are made from physics content independently. The §2.5 feedback-loop self-check applies to every authoring iteration.

The **§4 admissibility test** (Phase C scope memos and `MAP_v19_schema_spec_extension.md` Rules 22, 34–36) binds quantitative claims: literature-cited values, per-program-internal predictions, design-document sensitivities. No project-side computation enters the substrate.

The **literature-anchoring principle** (per `MENDELEEV_POSITIONING_HANDOFF.md` §7) binds substrate additions: substrate content is added only when the indexing or synthesis is itself proposed in the literature — not when the project would be synthesizing across literature-anchored fragments. This is the discipline that keeps M2's recurrence-recording on the substrate-contained side of the split and M2's recurrence-discovery on the substrate-enabled side.

This document — the **positioning frame** — binds the prose surface text downstream. The vocabulary supplement (`PHYSICIST_FACING_VOCABULARY.md`) governs how each surface adapts the frame's prose for its audience. The frame is the single canonical source; surfaces quote or adapt from it rather than re-inventing wording.
